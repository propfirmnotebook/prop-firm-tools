Profit Split Calculator

Know exactly what you will take home before you generate the profit.

A profit split determines how much of your generated profit goes to you versus the prop firm. Most firms offer splits between 70% and 90% for the trader. This calculator shows your actual payout, the firm's share, and net estimate after any fees or deductions.

Calculator

Example Walkthrough

If you generated $5,000 in profit with an 80% split, you receive $4,000 and the firm keeps $1,000. After deducting $50 in withdrawal fees, your net payout is $3,950. Always check for hidden fees like monthly platform fees or inactivity charges.

Common Payout Mistakes

  • Not accounting for monthly subscription fees when calculating net profit.
  • Assuming the profit split applies to all accounts simultaneously.
  • Ignoring minimum payout thresholds set by the firm.
  • Forgetting about withdrawal fees and processing times.

Checklist Before Requesting Payout

  • I know my exact profit split percentage from the firm's terms.
  • I have met all consistency and trading day requirements.
  • I have deducted all fees and subscription costs.
  • I understand the withdrawal schedule and processing time.

FAQ

What is a typical profit split?

Most prop firms offer between 70% and 90% for the trader. Top-tier firms sometimes offer up to 95% for consistent performers.

Are there hidden fees I should know about?

Some firms charge monthly platform fees, inactivity fees, or withdrawal fees. Always read the full terms before joining.

How often can I request payouts?

This varies by firm. Some offer weekly payouts, others monthly. Check the specific firm's payout schedule.

Do profit splits apply to all accounts?

Usually yes, but some firms have tiered systems where larger funded accounts or multiple accounts may have different split structures.
