Risk of Ruin Calculator

See how your win rate, risk per trade, and reward-to-risk ratio affect your long-term survival.

Risk of ruin measures the probability that your account will be wiped out over a series of trades. Even with a positive edge, too much risk per trade can lead to devastating losing streaks. This calculator shows your survival pressure and suggests safer risk levels.

Calculator

Example Walkthrough

A trader with a $100,000 account risking 1% per trade with a 50% win rate and 2:1 reward-to-risk has a positive expectancy. However, even with this edge, a string of losses can still cause significant drawdown. The calculator shows how reducing risk to 0.5% dramatically improves survival odds.

Common Mistakes

  • Risking more than 2% per trade in a challenge.
  • Overestimating win rate after a short sample.
  • Ignoring the impact of consecutive losses.
  • Increasing risk after a winning streak.

Checklist Before Trading

  • I know my actual win rate from recorded data.
  • I understand my true reward-to-risk ratio.
  • My risk per trade is small enough to survive 10 losses.
  • I am not increasing risk to chase targets.

FAQ

What is risk of ruin?

Risk of ruin is the statistical probability that a trading strategy will lose all capital over a given number of trades, based on win rate, risk per trade, and reward-to-risk ratio.

How much should I risk per trade?

Most professional traders risk between 0.25% and 1% per trade. Higher risk increases both potential gains and the chance of catastrophic losses.

Does a high win rate guarantee survival?

No. A high win rate with poor reward-to-risk can still lead to ruin if the occasional large loss wipes out many small wins.

How does reward-to-risk affect survival?

Higher reward-to-risk ratios mean you need a lower win rate to be profitable, which significantly improves long-term survival chances.
