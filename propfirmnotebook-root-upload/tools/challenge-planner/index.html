Prop Firm Challenge Planner

Break a challenge into calmer numbers before the evaluation begins.

A challenge plan helps you avoid forcing trades. It shows the target, the maximum loss amount, the average daily pace, and how many full-risk losses could put the account in danger.

Calculator

Example Walkthrough

On a $100,000 challenge with an 8% target, the target is $8,000. Over 20 trading days, that averages $400 per day. This is a planning guide, not a reason to trade every day.

Common Mistakes

  • Treating the average daily goal like a requirement.
  • Using too much risk early.
  • Increasing risk after falling behind.
  • Ignoring the maximum loss while chasing the target.

Checklist Before Starting

  • I know the profit target.
  • I know the maximum loss.
  • I know my normal risk per trade.
  • I have rules for stopping after losses.

FAQ

What is a prop firm challenge?

It is an evaluation where a trader tries to reach a profit target without breaking risk rules.

How do I plan a funded trader challenge?

Start with the target, maximum loss, daily loss, planned trading days, and risk per trade.

Should I force a daily profit target?

No. The average daily number is only a planning guide. Good traders skip bad setups.

What is the safest way to pass a challenge?

There is no guaranteed safe way, but smaller risk, clear rules, and avoiding revenge trading can improve survival.
